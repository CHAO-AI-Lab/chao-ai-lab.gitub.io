About the Lab

CHAO-AI Lab stands for Collaborative Hybrid Architectures for Open-ended Agentic Intelligence Lab.

We believe the next generation of AI systems will not come from a single model or a single algorithm, but from the careful design of architectures in which models, tools, data, knowledge, humans, and multiple agents collaborate. Our goal is to build AI systems that can understand language, retrieve and integrate knowledge, analyze heterogeneous data, reason over long horizons, interact with humans and other agents, and act reliably on open-ended tasks.

Research Vision

We organize our research around three principles that together form the name CHAO-AI.

C · Collaborative

Intelligence emerges from collaboration

Between humans and AI, between multiple agents, and between models, tools, and data systems. We design interaction protocols, communication mechanisms, and coordination strategies that make collaboration efficient, controllable, and trustworthy.

H · Hybrid

Heterogeneous components, coherent systems

Real-world intelligence integrates LLMs, retrieval, structured knowledge, tools, code execution, time-series analytics, and traditional ML. We study how to combine these into architectures that exploit the strengths of each.

AO · Architectures for Open-ended Agentic Intelligence

Architectures for open-ended agentic intelligence

We focus on architectures—not isolated tricks—that enable agents to plan, retrieve, analyze, decide, and act in environments where tasks are under-specified and information is incomplete.

Research Areas

From foundation models to deployable agentic systems, covering the complete technology stack for agentic AI.

Large Language Models & Reasoning

Reasoning, planning, long-context understanding, knowledge integration, and capability evaluation in complex tasks.

Agentic AI & Tool Use

LLM-based agents with planning, memory, reflection, tool calling, code execution, browser interaction, and long-horizon task completion.

Multi-Agent Systems & Interaction

Communication, coordination, role specialization, debate, and cooperation among agents for research, analysis, and decision support.

Retrieval-Augmented & Knowledge-Enhanced Systems

Information retrieval, RAG, structured knowledge integration, and grounding agents in trustworthy information sources.

Natural Language Processing

Language understanding, information extraction, semantic representation, and natural language interfaces for agentic systems.

Time-Series Mining & Data Analytics

Modeling and analysis of temporal and multi-source data, integrated into agentic workflows for insight discovery.

Human-AI Collaboration & Trustworthy Agents

Human-in-the-loop design, controllable agents, intent alignment, and interpretable, reliable behavior in open-ended settings.

Core Questions

Key questions driving our research, exploring the future development of agentic systems.

  • Reliable long-horizon agents

    How can LLMs become agents that act safely and reliably over long horizons in open-ended environments?

  • Hybrid architectures

    How do we combine LLMs, retrieval, tools, knowledge, and analytics so the whole is greater than the sum of its parts?

  • Multi-agent problem solving

    How can multiple agents communicate, coordinate, and specialize to solve problems no single agent could solve alone?

  • Human-AI collaboration

    How can humans and AI agents collaborate in research, analysis, and decision-making while keeping humans informed and in control?

  • Data-grounded agents

    How can agents truly understand temporal and analytical data—not just text—and turn raw data into trustworthy insights?
